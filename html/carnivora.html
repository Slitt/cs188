Carnivora (Latin: carō "flesh", vorāre "devour") is a mammalian order consisting of species that consume primarily flesh. Carnivorans (noun: members of the carnivora order) are massively significant to humans, being domesticated (dogs, cats, etc.) and represented culturally (lions having royalty, dogs having loyalty, etc.) in many civilizations throughout time.
